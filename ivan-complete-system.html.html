The SQ3R Method
Best Reading Technique
Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review. Used by top university students worldwide...
S Survey: Skim all headings in 2 minutes before reading. Get the big picture first.
Q Question: Turn each heading into a question. Now you read to find an answer.
R Read: Read actively to answer your questions. Notes in your own words only.
R Recite: Close the book and explain it out loud after each section.
R Review: Write 3 key points at end of each session. Review before the next session.

How to Approach Exams
Exam Strategy for University
Exams are not about how much you know — they are about how well you organize and present it...
4 Weeks Before: Create one summary page per topic in your own words. Forces understanding not memorization.
2 Weeks Before: Stop new material. Review summaries. Answer 5 years of past exam questions.
Night Before: Light review only. Sleep by 10pm. Sleep consolidates memory more than last-minute cramming.
In the Exam: Read ALL questions first. Start with what you know best. Never spend 30+ minutes on one question.
After Each Exam: Do not discuss answers. Shift focus immediately to the next paper.

Design Fundamentals
Level 1 Foundation
Before tools — understand the principles that make every great design work...
Color Theory: Complementary colors create contrast. Analogous colors create harmony. Limit yourself to 2-3 colors per design.
Typography: Never use more than 2 fonts in one design. One for headings bold and strong, one for body clean and readable.
Spacing White Space: Crowded designs look amateur. Give elements room to breathe. White space directs the eye.
Alignment: Everything should be aligned to something. Misalignment looks lazy. Use grids in Canva.
Hierarchy: The most important information should be biggest and boldest. Guide the viewers eye from most to least important.
